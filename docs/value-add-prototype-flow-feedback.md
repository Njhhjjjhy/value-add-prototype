#### Section 3 hard rules

Section 3 has tighter constraints than the rest because it does heavy strategic work in minimal space. Treat as constraints, not preferences.

**Required content.**
- Year anchor. The line "Kumamoto 2025 = Hsinchu 2007" or equivalent equation.
- One Hsinchu outcome number only. Baoshan +120% over 3 years.
- One Kumamoto data point only. Ozu Town +33% annual gain, #1 in Japan.
- Side-by-side photo pair. Same camera angle if possible.
- One bridge line, single sentence maximum.
- Forward-pointing closer that points to section 10.

**Forbidden content.**
- The full timeline. That is section 10.
- Mechanism explanations of why fabs drive housing demand.
- Additional data points beyond the two specified.
- MoreHarvest team, track record, or MOHA Intel content. That is section 11.
- Caveats, disclaimers, or hedging language.

**Target:** under 10 seconds of scroll. Conviction installed, not argued.

### Section 9. The four risks

**File:** `section_09_four_risks_hedges.html`
**Eyebrow:** Section 09 · Underwriting maturity
**Headline (two lines):** The four risks. / And how we hedge each.

**Risk 01 — Demand concentration:**
- Risk: If TSMC expansion slows or the semiconductor cycle softens, deployment volume could compress.
- Hedge: Tenant base extends beyond JASM to local supply chain (Sony, equipment vendors) and convertible to standard Japanese rental market.

**Risk 02 — Infrastructure timing:**
- Risk: If the new airport or surrounding infrastructure is delayed, area appeal and rent growth could lag.
- Hedge: We deploy only in zones with existing livability infrastructure. Underwriting reflects today's demand, not future promises.

**Risk 03 — Tenant concentration:**
- Risk: Over-reliance on a single corporate tenant could create vacancy and renewal-pricing risk.
- Hedge: Portfolio spread across multiple supply-chain firms. Product is generic enough to serve both deployed engineers and standard tenants.

**Risk 04 — Execution risk:**
- Risk: Construction overruns or timeline slippage directly compress IRR and capital velocity.
- Hedge: Standardized renovation specs eliminate customization variance. 1–2 month build window with fixed-scope contractors.
